A plain-English read on the Pakistan Stock Exchange and the halal stocks our AI is watching right now.

Market outlook

Pakistan's stock market has been on a good run lately, and the mood right now is upbeat and hopeful. The cheerful crowd thinks it can keep climbing because shares look cheap compared to other countries and a few worries have eased. But there's a real catch: the cost of borrowing money is still high and the price of everyday things has been rising, which could put a lid on how far the market goes.

  • A lot of the upbeat forecasts trace back to just a small handful of people, so take the cheeriest predictions with a grain of salt.
  • The bright case leans on shares simply being valued more generously over time, not on companies actually earning a lot more money right now.
  • Keep an eye on the central bank's next decision on borrowing costs next month — if they make borrowing cheaper, the market could get another lift, but if they hold steady or make it more expensive, the hopeful story gets a lot weaker.
