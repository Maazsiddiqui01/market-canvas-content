How it works

The calculator compounds your monthly contribution month by month at the return rate you choose. The orange line is your portfolio value; the grey dashed line is the cash you actually put in — the gap between them is compound growth. Small, consistent amounts started early beat large amounts started late, which is the whole point of compound interest and dollar-cost averaging.

Returns are illustrative — real markets rise and fall. A diversified, long-term approach (see risk and diversification) is how most people capture this growth. To invest the halal way, screen holdings with our halal stock screener.

Frequently asked questions

How does this investment calculator work?

It compounds your monthly investment at your chosen annual return, compounded monthly, over the number of years you set, then shows the future value, total invested, and the growth.

What return rate should I use?

It depends on what you invest in and is never guaranteed. Long-run stock-market averages are often cited around 8-12% before inflation, but use a conservative figure and remember markets fall as well as rise.

Why does starting early matter so much?

Because compounding builds on itself: early contributions have the most years to grow, so starting sooner usually beats investing more later.
