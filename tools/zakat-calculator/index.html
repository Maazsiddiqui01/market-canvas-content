How it works

Zakat is an annual act of worship: once your net wealth has stayed above the nisab (a minimum threshold) for one full lunar year, you give 2.5% of it. This calculator adds up your zakatable assets — cash and bank balances, the value of gold and silver, the market value of your stocks and investments, and business stock or money owed to you — then subtracts debts that are due now. If the net figure is at or above the nisab, it shows your 2.5% zakat.

The nisab is the value of either 87.48g of gold or 612.36g of silver. Many scholars in Pakistan favour the lower silver-based nisab because it brings more people into giving. To set it, enter today's price per gram of your chosen metal. For zakat specifically on gold, see our guide to calculating zakat on gold, and for shares see do you pay zakat on stocks?

Frequently asked questions

How much zakat do I pay?

Zakat is 2.5% of your net zakatable wealth (assets minus immediate debts), provided that wealth has stayed at or above the nisab for one full lunar year.

What is the nisab for zakat?

Nisab is the minimum wealth before zakat is due: the value of 87.48 grams of gold or 612.36 grams of silver. Use the current local price per gram of your chosen metal; many scholars prefer the lower silver figure.

Do I pay zakat on stocks and shares?

Yes — most scholars hold that shares held for trading are zakatable at 2.5% of their market value, and long-term holdings on the zakatable-asset portion. See our zakat-on-stocks guide for details.

Is this zakat calculator accurate?

It applies the standard 2.5% rate and nisab method, but zakat has nuances (e.g. gold jewellery in regular use) where scholars differ. Treat it as a helpful estimate and confirm your situation with a qualified scholar.
